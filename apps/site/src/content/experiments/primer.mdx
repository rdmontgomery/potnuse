Built for my daughter. She knows the alphabet — can name every letter cold — but the leap from naming to *reading* is its own ladder. So this is one: tap the picture that starts with this letter, or the picture that matches this word. Cards you miss return sooner; cards you land drift further out.

[Open the primer →](/experiments/primer)

## two rungs

**letters** is letter-sound matching. A big *A a* sits on the card; three pictures sit underneath. The right answer is the one whose name begins with that sound. The card itself is silent until you ask — tap the speaker and a voice says the sound, then the exemplar word. The point isn't the alphabet song. The point is the sound the letter makes when it's working.

**words** is short-word recognition. A three-letter word sits on the card; tap it and a voice sounds it out — *c... a... t... cat.* Then pick the picture. CVC words mostly: cat, dog, pig, sun, bug, hat, bus. The kind of words a child can decode the first day she figures out blending.

## why two modes and not more

Letter-naming and letter-sounding are the same skill in different clothes — she already names. Letter-sounding and word-reading are not. Sounding is a lookup; reading is a blend. The two modes are the two cliffs, not the whole climb.

Sight words come next, probably, when she's ready for *the*, *and*, *is* — words that don't decode cleanly and have to be memorized as shapes. They want a third mode rather than a third deck inside an existing one, because the feedback shape is different: no picture, no decoding, just the word and your voice naming it back.

## design notes

- **Pictures, not text, for answers.** A non-reader can't pick from `cat / dog / pig` written out — they'd be reading the answers. Emoji as low-cost iconography: no asset pipeline, recognizable, varied.
- **Audio is on demand, not automatic.** Auto-speech the moment a card appears trains the kid to wait for the voice instead of looking at the letter. The speak button is there for help; the card is the prompt.
- **Reinforcement after answer.** Right or wrong, the voice says what the card was. Wrong gets a longer pause before the next card so the correction lands.
- **Same SRS bones as [ladder](/experiments/ladder).** Leitner-style intervals, per-mode persistence to localStorage. Copy the bones; don't abstract them — the *shape* of a card here (prompt + three pictures) is different enough from naming a note that a shared engine would just be a thicker layer to pass everything through.
