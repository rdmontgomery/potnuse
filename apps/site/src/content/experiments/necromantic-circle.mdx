A graph of the dead I keep talking to. Each node is a thinker whose ideas still do work for me — the ones I reach for when a problem feels stuck. Domain color is where they published; the edges are what they *do* to each other inside my head: influence, bridge, tension, co-domain, collaboration, application, extension.

It's not a bibliography and it's not a canon. A canon wants completeness. This wants useful pressure. I'm looking for the places two unrelated domains touch — that's where I'd like to contribute something novel, if I'm going to contribute anything at all.

## why this is different from the rhizome

The rhizome graphs *this site* — pages and their `connects`. The necromantic circle graphs *the outside* — thinkers who inform the site but don't live in it. Same visual grammar on purpose: this site is downstream of that graph.

## how to read it

- **bridges** (gold dashed): the most valuable edge. Two thinkers in different domains doing the same structural move — Shannon and Boltzmann on entropy, Girard and Peirce on reasoning from circumstantial pattern.
- **tension** (red dashed): productive disagreement. Chalmers and Dennett. Rand and Peirce.
- **co-domain** (muted solid): same territory, different angles. Chalmers, Nagel, and Block on consciousness.
- **influenced** / **extends** / **applies** / **collaborated**: the ordinary lineage edges.

It also persists — add a node (`+ summon`), draw an edge (`+ connect`), and it sticks in `localStorage`. Reset blows it away.

## open questions

- The hardest thing is honesty about edges. It's easy to feel a resonance and hard to name the structural move that actually connects two thinkers. I expect half the current bridges will collapse under a second pass.
- Should this eventually fold into the toner-tu map? Probably not — the site map wants recency/attention as drift velocity; this wants a different order parameter (proximity to unresolved questions, maybe).
- Who's missing? The glaring holes I can see today: Deleuze & Guattari (the rhizome is theirs), Simondon (individuation as the thing being graphed), Levi-Strauss (structural anthropology as the ur-method). Haven't added them yet because I don't trust my own read of them enough to name an edge.
